Bio
Anthony J. Abbruzzi, DO, is certified by the American College of Osteopathic Internists.

In addition to serving on the staff at Aria Health, Dr. Abbruzzi is medical director of Evercare Hospice and Palliative Care in King of Prussia, PA, where he oversees the care of all hospice patients and assists with complicated pain management and end-of-life circumstances.

Dr. Abbruzzi received a bachelor’s degree from Ursinus College in Collegeville, PA, and a medical degree from the Philadelphia College of Osteopathic Medicine, in Philadelphia, PA. He completed an internship and residency at Philadelphia College of Osteopathic Medicine and its affiliated hospitals.

Dr. Abbruzzi is a fellow of the American College of Osteopathic Internists, and is a member of the American Osteopathic Association, the Pennsylvania Osteopathic Medical Association, and the American College of Osteopathic Internists.

Bio
Amy Abouzied, MD, FACOG, graduated from Michigan State University with a Bachelor’s degree in Medical Microbiology and Immunology. She received her medical training at Wayne State University School of Medicine in Detroit, MI. She went on to complete her Obstetrics and Gynecology Residency at University of Maryland in Baltimore, and continued her training at the Department of Obstetrics and Gynecology at Mercy Medical Center with a Fellowship in Endoscopy and Pelvic Surgery in 2012.

Dr. Abouzied is a Junior Fellow at the American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists and a member of the American Association of Gynecologists and Laparoscopists.

Before joining Aria Health, Dr. Abouzied was practicing in the Chicago area. She has a special interest in treating endometriosis, pelvic prolapse, and incontinence issues. In addition, Dr. Abouzied has experience in offering patients a multitude of minimally invasive surgical techniques. Her philosophy is to develop a strong and trusting relationship with her patients so they feel as comfortable as possible with their gynecological care, options, and decisions.
